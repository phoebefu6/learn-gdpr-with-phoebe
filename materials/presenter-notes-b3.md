# Presenter notes - Practitioner session 3: Privacy notices + transparency

**Session goal (one line).** Give practitioners the Article 13/14 content checklist and the layered-notice pattern, and get them auditing a real notice against it.

## Run of show (45 min)

- 0-3 - Welcome. Frame it: transparency is the GDPR part the public actually sees, and the cheapest to get materially wrong.

- 3-18 - Concepts (15 min). Part 1: the content checklist, Article 13 (direct) vs 14 (indirect, plus the source), timing rules, and the PDPA Notification parallel.

- 18-40 - Build-along (22 min). Part 2: Article 12 clear-and-plain language and the layered notice; the notice as the front door to the rights (self-study). Then the "audit a real notice" exercise, pen and paper.

- 40-45 - Q&A and Lumina-after-b3 recap.

## Preflight

- Open b3-privacy-notices.html, Expand all, test Projector zoom.

- Have the notice-anatomy SVG and the layered-notice SVG ready.

- Bring a real privacy notice (the org's own or a public one) to audit live against the checklist.

## Never-cut beats

- The three most-commonly-missing-but-required items: the lawful basis, the retention period, and the right to complain to a supervisory authority.

- Article 13 (collected from the person) needs the notice at collection; Article 14 (from elsewhere) adds telling them the source.

- Completeness is not transparency - Article 12 demands clear, plain language, which is why the layered notice (short summary over full detail) exists.

## Cuts if running long

- Drop the "notice as the front door to the rights" self-study card.

- Trim the audit exercise to the checklist tick and the missing-items circle.

## Quiz answers

1. C - The lawful basis, retention period, and the right to complain to an authority - commonly missing but required by Article 13.

2. A - The source the data came from; Article 14 mirrors 13 but adds the source since the person did not provide it directly.

3. B - The layered notice reconciles "complete" with "clear and plain" - a short summary linking to the full detail.

## Common questions + crisp answers

- "Is our cookie banner our privacy notice?" No - a banner listing cookies is not the Article 13 notice; the notice must name the controller, purposes, lawful basis, retention, rights, and the complaint right.

- "How soon must we inform people when we got data from a third party?" Within a reasonable period, at the latest one month, or at first contact (Article 14).

- "Where does the notice have to appear?" At the point of collection - a link on the signup form and checkout, not buried three clicks deep in a footer.

## Compliance caution

Educational, not legal advice. Verify a real notice against the Regulation text and get advice before publishing - a generated or templated notice that does not match your actual data flows looks compliant while being wrong.
