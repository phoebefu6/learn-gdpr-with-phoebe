# Presenter notes - Practitioner session 5: Records + DPIA

**Session goal (one line).** Get practitioners building a record-of-processing entry they can replicate, killing the "small company is exempt" myth, and knowing when a DPIA is required.

## Run of show (45 min)

- 0-3 - Welcome. Frame the ROPA as the least glamorous, most load-bearing artifact - almost every other task reads from it.

- 3-18 - Concepts (15 min). Part 1: the ROPA fields, the under-250 exemption and its three carve-outs (especially "not occasional"), and why the ROPA pays for itself (DSARs, transfers, breach scope, accountability evidence).

- 18-40 - Build-along (22 min). Part 2: the DPIA trigger test and the three examples (Art 35), timing (before launch), contents; the DPIA as a design tool (self-study). Then the "draft Lumina's records + a DPIA call" exercise.

- 40-45 - Q&A and Lumina-after-b5 recap.

## Never-cut beats

- The under-250 exemption (Art 30(5)) almost never applies - "not occasional" catches any regular business processing, so treat records as required.

- The ROPA is the master inventory - a DSAR search, a transfer map, and a breach scope all read from it, and it is your Art 5(2) accountability evidence.

- A DPIA is a pre-flight check done BEFORE the high-risk processing starts, not a post-mortem.

## Cuts if running long

- Drop the "DPIA as a design tool" self-study card.

- Trim the exercise to two ROPA rows plus the DPIA call on the pricing-tier feature.

## Quiz answers

1. C - Almost never exempt; the under-250 exemption falls away when processing is "not occasional", which routine business processing is.

2. A - A DPIA is required when processing is likely to result in a high risk, e.g. large-scale profiling with significant effects.

3. B - Before the processing starts - a DPIA done after launch merely documents a risk already taken.

## Common questions + crisp answers

- "We are tiny - do we really need a ROPA?" Almost certainly yes. The three carve-outs (risk to rights, not occasional, special-category data) cancel the exemption for nearly every operating company.

- "Who has to keep records - just controllers?" Both controllers and processors must keep records (Art 30(1) and 30(2)).

- "What goes in a DPIA?" A description of the processing, an assessment of necessity and proportionality, the risks to individuals, and the measures that reduce them.

## Compliance caution

Educational, not legal advice. Verify against the Regulation for real DPIAs, and note that "large-scale" and "high risk" are judgement calls - authorities publish their own must-DPIA lists to check against.
