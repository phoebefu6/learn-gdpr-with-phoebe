# Presenter notes - Practitioner session 6: Transfers in practice

**Session goal (one line).** Turn the transfer mechanisms into a repeatable procedure - SCCs, a transfer impact assessment, and supplementary measures - and cover the sub-processors people forget.

## Run of show (45 min)

- 0-3 - Welcome. Frame it as the single highest-value GDPR task a SG company does - it is the failure behind the largest fine ever.

- 3-18 - Concepts (15 min). Part 1: putting SCCs in place (who signs, correct module, annexes) and the transfer impact assessment (what it asks, then measures, document it).

- 18-40 - Build-along (22 min). Part 2: covering sub-processors and reusing the b5 ROPA transfers column; localisation as sometimes the simplest fix (self-study). Then the "lawful transfers for Lumina" exercise.

- 40-45 - Q&A and Lumina-after-b6 recap.

## Never-cut beats

- The modern transfer is three moves: sign SCCs, run a TIA on the destination, add measures where the assessment finds a gap - skipping the middle move is what Schrems II forbids.

- The strongest supplementary measure is technical - strong encryption with keys held outside the destination country - because it protects the data even if local law reaches the server.

- Every sub-processor outside the EU that touches EU data is a transfer you own; the ROPA transfers column is your list, and a blank is an unlawful transfer.

## Quiz answers

1. B - SCCs, plus a transfer impact assessment, plus supplementary measures where needed; post-Schrems II, SCCs alone are not enough.

2. C - Strong encryption with keys held outside the destination country - technical measures carry the most weight.

3. A - A transfer you are responsible for; every sub-processor outside the EU that touches EU data needs its own mechanism.

## Common questions + crisp answers

- "Is signing SCCs enough now?" No - Schrems II requires a transfer impact assessment of the destination's law plus supplementary measures where it falls short.

- "Where should our encryption keys live?" Outside the destination country - keys held in Singapore weaken the whole supplementary-measure argument.

- "Is there a way to avoid all this?" Sometimes - keeping EU customer data in an EU cloud region removes the transfer entirely for that data. Weigh it against the operational cost.

## Compliance caution

Educational, not legal advice. SCC versions and adequacy status change - verify the current position and get advice before relying on a transfer mechanism.
