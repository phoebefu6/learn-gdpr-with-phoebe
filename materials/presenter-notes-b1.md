# Presenter notes - Practitioner session 1: Does GDPR apply? (the gdpr-live checker)

**Session goal (one line).** Turn Article 3 into a thirty-second reflex using the live checker, and give practitioners the first three actions the moment a product is found in scope.

## Run of show (45 min)

- 0-3 - Welcome. Frame applicability as the gate: no point picking a basis or writing a notice until you know GDPR applies.

- 3-18 - Concepts (15 min). Part 1: what each of the three triggers really asks, and the close call ("offering" turns on targeting, not accessibility); the first three actions in scope (lawful basis, EU rep, record of processing).

- 18-40 - Build-along (22 min). Part 2: drive the live checker in applicability mode (answer no/yes/yes for Lumina, watch it cite Art 3(2)(a), then flip an answer), then basis mode. Then the "screen your real products" exercise on the checker.

- 40-45 - Q&A and the Lumina-after-b1 recap.

## Preflight

- Open b1-applicability.html, scroll to the .gdprbox, and change a dropdown to confirm the verdict + article render (gdpr-live.js loaded).

- Run the checker in both modes (apply, basis) once before class so nothing surprises you live.

- Expand all, test Projector zoom, and have a real product or campaign ready to screen.

## Never-cut beats

- Any one "yes" to the three triggers means in scope; for a SG company it usually turns on offering or monitoring.

- Accessibility is not offering - the trigger needs evidence of targeting, and the checker is a teaching model, not a legal ruling.

- "Out of scope" is a moving target - re-run the test on a new market, new analytics, or a big EU customer.

## Cuts if running long

- Drop the basis-mode build-along here (session b2 covers it in full) and keep applicability mode.

- Trim the "screen your real products" exercise to one product plus the reflection line.

## Quiz answers

1. B - Any one of the three Article 3 triggers is enough to bring you into scope.

2. C - Mere accessibility is probably NOT enough; the trigger turns on evidence of targeting.

3. A - Establish a lawful basis for the EU data you already process and start a record of processing (plus likely an EU representative).

## Common questions + crisp answers

- "The checker said no - are we safe forever?" No. It reflects what your business does today; a new EU market or a big EU customer flips it. Re-run at those moments.

- "It said we are in scope - what first?" Set a lawful basis for the EU data, appoint an EU representative (Art 27) unless exempt, and start the Article 30 record of processing.

- "Does the checker decide it for us?" No - it teaches the structure of the test. Close calls, especially "offering to the EU", need the Regulation and advice.

## Compliance caution

Educational, not legal advice. The checker is a teaching model of Articles 3 and 6 - stress that the "offering to people in the EU" limb turns on evidence of targeting, and real decisions need proper advice.
