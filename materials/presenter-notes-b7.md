# Presenter notes - Practitioner session 7: Breach response

**Session goal (one line).** Build a timed, owned breach response - contain, assess, notify within 72 hours, remediate - that clears the clock even on a weekend.

## Run of show (45 min)

- 0-3 - Welcome. Frame it: everything else in the track was preparation; this is the day it is tested, and the clock starts before you are ready.

- 3-18 - Concepts (15 min). Part 1: the notifiable test and two clocks (authority any risk within 72h, individuals if high risk), the processor duty; the PDPA contrast for a dual-regime breach.

- 18-40 - Build-along (22 min). Part 2: what to include, when to consult first, closing the loop; the response plan is the deliverable (self-study). Then the "run Lumina's breach clock" exercise, timed.

- 40-45 - Q&A and Lumina-after-b7 recap.

## Never-cut beats

- The 72-hour clock starts when you become AWARE and runs through weekends - it does not wait for the investigation.

- Two thresholds: any risk to the authority within 72h (Art 33); high risk also to individuals (Art 34), with the encryption exception (the payoff from b6).

- The real bottleneck is organisational, not technical - a single incident owner with pre-granted authority to notify is the fix.

## Quiz answers

1. B - The clock starts when you become aware of the breach (Art 33); it runs through weekends and does not wait for the investigation.

2. C - Notify individuals when the breach is likely to result in a high risk to their rights (Art 34); strong encryption can remove that duty.

3. A - The approval step - nobody is sure they are authorised to notify; a single owner with pre-granted authority is the fix.

## Common questions + crisp answers

- "What if we do not have all the facts in 72 hours?" You can notify in phases - file what you have and follow up as the investigation develops.

- "We are a processor, not the controller - what do we do?" Notify the controller you work for without undue delay; you are a link in their 72 hours.

- "When is individual notice not required?" When the affected data was strongly encrypted, among other exceptions - which is exactly why the b6 measures matter.

## Compliance caution

Educational, not legal advice. Have real incident-response advice in place before you need it, and rehearse the plan (a tabletop) rather than drafting it during a live incident.
