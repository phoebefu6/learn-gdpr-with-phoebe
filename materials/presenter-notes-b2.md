# Presenter notes - Practitioner session 2: Picking a lawful basis (basis-mode checker)

**Session goal (one line).** Get practitioners matching the right lawful basis to each purpose, treating consent and legitimate interests with the care each needs, and documenting the choice.

## Run of show (45 min)

- 0-3 - Welcome. Open blunt: no basis, no processing - and consent is one door of six, often the weakest.

- 3-18 - Concepts (15 min). Part 1: match the basis to the purpose, the three you actually use (contract, legitimate interests, consent), the anti-pattern of one consent checkbox for everything. Include the first basis-mode build-along here.

- 18-40 - Build-along (22 min). Part 2: the consent standard (Art 4(11), 7) and the three-part LIA; special-category data needs Art 9 too; "why you cannot swap bases later" (self-study). Then the "assign Lumina's bases" exercise on the checker.

- 40-45 - Q&A and Lumina-after-b2 recap.

## Never-cut beats

- Do not default to consent - if processing is necessary for the contract, consent is the wrong, fragile choice.

- Legitimate interests needs a documented three-part balancing test (interest, necessity, balance against rights); consent needs a real, demonstrable, withdrawable opt-in.

- Special-category data needs an Article 9 condition on top of the Article 6 basis - two locks, not one.

## Cuts if running long

- Drop the "why you cannot swap bases later" self-study card.

- Trim the exercise to account/billing (contract) and marketing (consent), set analytics as homework.

## Quiz answers

1. C - Contract (Art 6(1)(b)) - processing the address is necessary to deliver what they bought; consent here would be fragile.

2. B - You must document a balancing test (an LIA): the interest, its necessity, and the balance against the person's rights.

3. A - An Article 6 basis is not enough on its own for special-category data; you also need an Article 9 condition.

## Common questions + crisp answers

- "Can we switch from consent to legitimate interests later if people withdraw?" No - you are held to the basis you picked and disclosed; a change of basis is a real decision with its own notice.

- "How many banners do we actually need?" Fewer than most teams run - move routine analytics to legitimate interests with an LIA and keep consent for genuine marketing.

- "What counts as valid consent?" Freely given, specific, informed, unambiguous, a clear affirmative act - never a pre-ticked box or silence - and as easy to withdraw as to give.

## Compliance caution

Educational, not legal advice, and the checker is a teaching model of Article 6. Note explicitly that "LIA" is a method name (regulator terminology from the ICO/EDPB), not statutory wording in the Regulation - teach it as the recognised method, not a quoted legal term.
