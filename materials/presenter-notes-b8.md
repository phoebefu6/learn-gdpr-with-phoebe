# Presenter notes - Practitioner session 8: The GDPR program + PDPA harmony

**Session goal (one line).** Turn seven capabilities into one standing programme that satisfies both the GDPR and the PDPA by building to the stricter of the two on each dimension.

## Run of show (45 min)

- 0-3 - Welcome. Frame it: seven sessions made seven capabilities; left separate they decay. A programme with an owner, living documents, and a cadence keeps them alive.

- 3-18 - Concepts (15 min). Part 1: the artifact checklist (owner, policies + notice, ROPA, DPIAs, transfers, DSAR flow, breach plan, training, review) and SME help worth using (PDPC/IMDA kits, EU SCC templates).

- 18-40 - Build-along (22 min). Part 2: the stricter-of-two rule point by point, one data map / one owner / one set of pipelines; compliance as a habit not a project (self-study). Then the "score your programme + 90-day plan" exercise.

- 40-45 - Q&A and the graduation recap - Lumina, and them.

## Never-cut beats

- Accountability (Art 5(2)) says you must be able to demonstrate compliance - the programme is that demonstration.

- Stricter of the two is genuinely point-by-point: GDPR is stricter on most things (basis, rights, breach clock, transfers), but the PDPA's universal DPO rule is tougher.

- One of each - one ROPA, one rights pipeline, one suppression list, one breach plan, one owner - not a "GDPR set" and a "PDPA set".

## Cuts if running long

- Drop the "SME help worth using" card (leave it as a homework pointer).

- Trim the exercise to the artifact scorecard plus naming the top-three gaps.

## Quiz answers

1. B - Build one programme to the stricter of the two on each dimension; meet that bar and you satisfy both.

2. C - The DPO - the PDPA requires one for every organisation, the GDPR only in defined cases, so the PDPA is stricter here.

3. A - It is built once and then not reviewed as the business changes; compliance is a habit, not a project.

## Common questions + crisp answers

- "So we just do GDPR and the PDPA follows?" Mostly, but not entirely - the PDPA's universal DPO requirement is stricter, so apply "stricter of the two" dimension by dimension.

- "How often should we review?" Quarterly is common, tied to the moments that change your data footprint: a new market, a new tool, a new feature.

- "Can we reuse the free templates?" Yes as scaffolding (PDPC/IMDA kits, EU SCC templates), but adapt them to your real flows - a generated notice that does not match reality is worse than none.

## Compliance caution

Educational, not legal advice. Verify against both the GDPR and the PDPA and get proper advice when designing a real compliance programme - and revisit the b1 applicability checker whenever the business changes, since scope is never permanent.
