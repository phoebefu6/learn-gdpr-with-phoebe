Learn GDPR with Phoebe

Two tracks on EU privacy, for an organisation that is not in the EU

The GDPR does not stop at Europe's border. Sell to, or track, people in the EU and it reaches your Singapore company - with fines up to 4% of global turnover. A leader track teaches what the GDPR requires and when it applies to you, mapped to the PDPA you already know. A practitioner track makes it operational: pick a lawful basis, write a compliant notice, handle a data-subject request, move data across borders, and answer a breach in 72 hours. The running case is Lumina, a Singapore SaaS with EU customers.

The leader track 🤝 · for C-level, managers & DPOs · 6 x 45 min · no jargon

What the GDPR requires, when it reaches a non-EU company, and how each piece maps to the PDPA you already run - each session ends with questions to ask your DPO and legal counsel.

🌍Leader 1 · easy

Why GDPR reaches a Singapore company

The Article 3 territorial-scope test - offer to or monitor people in the EU and you are in scope, no EU office required. And how that differs from the PDPA.

▶ Leaders start here 📜Leader 2 · easy

Seven principles, six lawful bases

The principles every processing must satisfy, and the six lawful bases - why consent is only one of them, unlike the PDPA's consent-first model.

Art 5-6 🙋Leader 3 · medium

Data-subject rights vs PDPA

Access, erasure, portability, objection and more - a broader set than the PDPA's access + correction. Which rights have no PDPA equivalent.

Art 12-22 🔗Leader 4 · medium

Controller, processor, DPO, DPIA

Who is responsible for what, when a DPO is mandatory (narrower than the PDPA), and when a high-risk project needs an impact assessment.

Accountability 🚨Leader 5 · deciding

Breach (72h) + penalties

The 72-hour clock, the two-tier fines (up to 4% of global turnover), and the billion-euro cases - versus the PDPA's own regime.

Real fines 🌐Leader 6 · deciding

International transfers

Adequacy, Standard Contractual Clauses, and the post-Schrems II duty to assess the destination - the rules that govern moving EU data to Singapore.
